History of Rugby

Did you know? Rugby originated in England in the early 19th century when William Webb Ellis decided to pick up a soccer ball and run with it!

Fun Fact: The Rugby World Cup trophy is named after William Webb Ellis.

Rules of Rugby

Rugby is played with two teams of 15 players. Points are scored through tries, conversions, penalty kicks, and drop goals. The ball can only be passed backward, and players must tackle opponents to stop their progress. The game emphasizes teamwork, strategy, and physical endurance.

Basic Rules

  1. Team Composition
    • 15 Players per Team
    • 8 Forwards and 7 Backs
  2. Scoring Points
    • Try: 5 points
    • Conversion: 2 points
    • Penalty Kick: 3 points
    • Drop Goal: 3 points
  3. Game Duration: 80 minutes

Rugby Positions

Forwards

Forwards are responsible for winning possession of the ball and are typically larger, stronger players.

Backs

Backs are faster, more agile players who focus on ball movement and scoring opportunities.

Key Roles

Some key roles include the Hooker, Scrum-half, Fly-half, and Fullback.
